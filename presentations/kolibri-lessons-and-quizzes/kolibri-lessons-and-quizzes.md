# Lessons, Quizzes and Progress in Kolibri
## Assigning content, testing understanding, tracking progress — Weeks 2–5

You have already created your class and built your first lesson. This presentation covers the full teaching cycle in Kolibri: assigning richer lessons, testing students with quizzes, and reading the progress data to know who needs support.

---

# The Teaching Cycle in Kolibri

```
Build a lesson  →  Students complete it  →  Create a quiz  →  Review the results  →  Adjust
```

Each step gives you more information about your students. By Week 5 you will be able to see exactly who understood the content and who needs more help.

---

# Building a Richer Lesson (Week 2)

You built a lesson last week. Now let us make it more complete.

1. Open your class → **Lessons** tab → open your existing lesson
2. Click **Add resources**
3. Search by topic or browse the channel
4. Add a mix of resource types if available:
   - A **video** to introduce the topic
   - An **exercise** to practise
   - A **document** for reference
5. Drag resources into the right order: video first, then exercise
6. Click **Save**

> **Principle:** A lesson should have a beginning (explain), a middle (practise), and a check (exercise or quiz). Kolibri can support all three.

---

# Assigning the Lesson to Your Class

A lesson must be assigned to your class before students can access it.

1. Open the lesson
2. Check that **Recipients** shows your class name
3. If not: click **Edit**, add your class under Recipients, save
4. Toggle the lesson to **Visible**

Students will see it immediately when they next open Kolibri.

---

# What Students Experience

When a student opens their assigned lesson:

- Resources appear in the order you set
- Videos play directly in the browser — no download needed
- Exercises ask questions and give immediate feedback
- Kolibri records: started / completed / score (for exercises)

The student does not need to do anything special — Kolibri tracks everything automatically in the background.

---

# Creating a Quiz (Week 4)

A **Quiz** lets you formally test understanding. It is built from exercises already in the channels.

1. Open your class → **Quizzes** tab
2. Click **+ New quiz**
3. Give it a name — e.g. `Week 4 Fractions Check`
4. Click **Add questions**
5. Browse the channels and select exercise sections to draw questions from
6. Set the number of questions _(start with 5–10)_
7. Click **Finish** — Kolibri randomly selects questions from your chosen pool
8. Toggle the quiz to **Active** when you are ready for students to take it

> **Note:** Quizzes can only be built from channels that contain exercises (not video-only channels). Tapiwa will confirm which channels on your system support this.

---

# Running the Quiz in Class

Once the quiz is Active:

1. Students log in to Kolibri → open their class → click **Quizzes**
2. They see the quiz and click **Start**
3. They answer each question and submit
4. Kolibri records their score automatically

You do not need to collect or mark anything. When students are done, you can view results immediately.

---

# Reading the Reports: Lesson Progress

After students have used Kolibri, check the Reports panel.

1. Click **Reports** in the left sidebar
2. Click **Classes** → select your class
3. Click **Lessons** → select the lesson

You will see a table: one row per student. For each student:

| Column | What it means |
|--------|--------------|
| Status | Not started / In progress / Completed |
| Progress | % of resources opened |
| Time spent | Total time on the lesson |

Students who have not started: follow up — did they not have time, or could they not find it?

---

# Reading the Reports: Quiz Results

1. Click **Reports** → **Classes** → your class
2. Click **Quizzes** → select the quiz

You will see:

| Column | What it means |
|--------|--------------|
| Score | % correct (e.g. 7/10) |
| Status | Completed / Not started |

Click on any student's name to see which specific questions they got wrong.

**What to do with this:**
- Students scoring below 50%: revisit the topic with them; assign a follow-up lesson
- Questions that most students got wrong: the whole class may need this explained again
- Share the pattern with Tapiwa — note it in the field report for Koen and Justice

---

# The Full Progress View (Week 5)

By Week 5 you have several weeks of data. Use the full class overview.

1. **Reports → Classes → [your class]**
2. Click **Learners** — you see every student with their overall activity

For each student you can see:
- Which lessons they have completed
- Their quiz scores over time
- Last time they used Kolibri

**Discussion questions for Week 5 with Tapiwa:**
- Which 2–3 students are furthest behind?
- What will you do differently for them next cycle?
- Which content did students engage with most? (most time spent, highest completion)

---

# Tips for Using This Data

**Do not chase 100% completion.** Some students work faster than others. Focus on whether there is genuine engagement — are students watching, attempting exercises, completing quizzes?

**One number to watch weekly:** quiz average score. If the class average drops from one week to the next, something got harder or less practiced.

**Share one insight per visit.** In the field report, note one thing you learned from the data — e.g. "Grade 5A completed the fractions lesson but 60% scored below 50% on the quiz — teacher wants to revisit."

# Week 6 — Plan Your Own Lesson

You have now used Kolibri for a full cycle. The next step is to make it yours.

**With Tapiwa's support, do the following before the end of this visit:**

1. Choose **one topic from your curriculum** for next term
2. Browse the Kolibri channels and find **2–3 resources** that cover it
3. Build a lesson using those resources and assign it to your class
4. Give the lesson a name that matches the curriculum topic — e.g. `Term 2 – Grade 5 Fractions`

> This is not a practice run. This is a real lesson you will use next term.

**Why this matters:**
Kolibri works best when it supports what you are already teaching. You know your curriculum — Kolibri has the content. Connecting the two is your job as the teacher. Tapiwa can help you find resources, but the choice of topic is yours.

**If you cannot find good resources for your chosen topic:**
- Try a related topic or a different grade level — the content may be there under a different name
- Note the gap in the field report — Koen and Justice will follow up on whether additional channels can be added

| Visit | What to check in Kolibri |
|-------|--------------------------|
| Week 1 | Class created, students enrolled, first lesson visible |
| Week 2 | Lesson assigned; preview Reports — no student data yet |
| Week 3 | Reports → Lessons: who has started; show teacher the progress view |
| Week 4 | Quiz results: aggregate scores; identify who needs support |
| Week 5 | Full overview: lesson completion + quiz scores + time spent per student |
| Week 6 | Review results; teacher plans 1 lesson from their own curriculum using Kolibri resources |
