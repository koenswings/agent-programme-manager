# Using Wikipedia Offline
## Finding information without the internet — Weeks 1–3

Wikipedia is an encyclopaedia. It contains articles on almost every subject: science, history, geography, health, mathematics, and more. On the IDEA system it is available without an internet connection — it runs directly from the server in your classroom.

---

# What Is Wikipedia?

Wikipedia is written and checked by thousands of people around the world. It is available in many languages, including simple English.

**What it is good for:**
- Background reading on a topic you are teaching
- Looking up definitions, facts, and explanations
- Finding diagrams and images to support a lesson
- Giving students a starting point for research

**What it is not:**
- A replacement for a textbook or curriculum guide
- A source you should copy word for word — read it, then explain it in your own words

# Searching for a Topic

From the Wikipedia home page:

1. Click the **search bar** at the top of the page
2. Type your topic — e.g. `photosynthesis` or `Zimbabwe history`
3. Press **Enter** or click the search icon
4. A list of matching articles will appear — click the one you want

**Tips:**
- Use simple, clear words — the same words you would use to explain the topic to a student
- If your first search gives no results, try a different word — e.g. `plants` instead of `photosynthesis`
- Results are shown in English by default; a language selector may be available depending on what is installed on your system

# Reading an Article

Each Wikipedia article has the same structure:

| Part | What it contains |
|------|-----------------|
| Opening paragraph | A short plain-language summary of the topic |
| Table of contents | Sections of the article — click to jump to a section |
| Body sections | Detailed information with headings |
| Images and diagrams | Visual content relevant to the article |

> **For classroom use:** The opening paragraph is often enough. You do not need to read the whole article — find the section that matches your lesson topic.

---

# Using Wikipedia in Your Lessons

Wikipedia works best as a **supplement** to what you are already teaching — not as the main lesson.

**Ways to use it:**

- **Before class:** Find an article on today's topic. Read the first two paragraphs. Use them to introduce the topic to students.
- **During class:** Open an article on the classroom screen. Point to a diagram or image. Ask students: "What do you see? What does this show?"
- **Student research task:** Assign students a topic. Ask them to find one Wikipedia article about it and write down three facts. Review together.

# If Wikipedia Does Not Load

Wikipedia requires the server to be running on the IDEA system.

1. First check that **Kolibri and Nextcloud are also loading** — if none of them load, the server is down (see the Troubleshooting Guide)
2. If Kolibri and Nextcloud load but Wikipedia does not, the Wikipedia app disk may not be docked — Tapiwa to check
3. If uncertain, note it in the field report for Koen to investigate remotely
